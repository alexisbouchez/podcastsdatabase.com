9. Recommendations

For podcastsdatabase.com specifically

Keep your Local pipeline for one-off episodes. At $0.17/hr with the pyannote subscription you already have, it's competitive with managed APIs and gives you the best Whisper model (large-v3-turbo).

Consider AssemblyAI for simplicity. At $0.17/hr with diarization included, it matches your Local pipeline cost but requires zero infrastructure — one API call returns transcription + diarization + timestamps. Their $50 free credit covers ~290 episodes to evaluate.

Consider Deepgram Nova-3 for batch. At $0.26/hr with the $200 free credit (~750 hrs), you could transcribe a massive back-catalog for free, with diarization included. Faster than Fly.io and no machines to manage.

Groq is the dark horse. At $0.04/hr for transcription, it's absurdly cheap and fast (22 seconds for a 1-hr episode). The catch: no diarization. But you already have a working diarize.ts script that pairs with pyannote.ai. A Groq + pyannote hybrid could replace your Local pipeline's Whisper step with a 164× speedup.

Retire Fly.io when A10s deprecate. Your Fly.io pipeline costs $0.39–$0.64/hr — 2–3× more than AssemblyAI or Deepgram. Its value is parallel batch processing, but managed APIs handle parallelism natively. If you migrate, Modal on a T4 ($0.15–$0.25/hr) is the closest self-hosted alternative with better DX than Fly Machines.

Avoid AWS/Google/Azure for this use case. At $0.96–$1.44/hr they're 5–8× more expensive than the best alternatives, with no accuracy advantage.
